Came across a beautiful ghazal attributed to Bahadur Shah Zafar (might have been written by somebody else).
Apparently written by him during his exile. It has been sung by various singers, I liked the one by [Habib Wali Mohammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habib_Wali_Mohammad) the best:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TK3gv-CeGq4

<i>Na kisi ki aankh ka noor hoon, na kisi ke dil ka qaraar hoon<br>
Jo kisi ke kaam na aa sake, mein vo ek musht-e-gubaar hoon

Mein nahin hoon naghamaa-e-jaan feza, koi sun ke mujhko karega kya<br>
Mein bade bikrog ki hoon sadaa, mein bade dukhon ki pukaar hoon<br>
(sung as: kisi dil-jale ki pukaar hoon)

Mera rang roop bigad gaya, mera yaar mujhse bichad gaya<br>
Jo chaman khizan se ujad gaya, mein usi ki fasl-e-bahaar hoon

Na to mein kisi ka habeeb hoon, na to mein kisi raqib hoon<br>
Jo bigad gaya vo naseeb hoon, jo ujad gaya vo dayaar hoon

Pae faatihaa koi aaye kyon, koi chaar phool chadhaaye kyon<br>
Koi aake shamaa jalaae kyon, mein vo bekasi kaa mazhaar hoon<br>
(Sung as: 'Zafar' ashq koi bahaaye kyon, ke mein bekasi ka mazaar hoon)</i>

-Bahadur Shah Zafar

It has been translated to English by a Russian student of Urdu poetry, [Philip Nikolayev](http://sundeepdougal.tripod.com/Zafar.html), :).

not the light of any one's eyes,<br>
nor the solace for any one's heart<br>
of no use to anyone,<br>
I am that one fistful of dust

I am not the song infusing life,<br>
why would anyone want to hear me<br>
I am the sound of separation,<br>
I am the wail of much distress

my complexion and beauty is ravaged,<br>
my beloved is parted from me<br>
the garden that got ruined in autumn,<br>
I am the crop of its spring

I am neither anyone's friend,<br>
nor am I anyone's rival<br>
the one that is ruined, I am that fate<br>
the one that is destroyed, that land

why should anyone come to sing a requiem<br>
why should anyone come to offer four flowers<br>
why should anyone come to light a candle<br>
I am the tomb of that destitution
