In this post, we put forth our understanding of Chomsky‘s opinion on
journalistic ethics. In the [linked interview with Andrew Marr](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pV85306K64w)
([Full interview](https://archive.org/details/noam-chomsky-1996-02-14-vs-andrew-marr-of-the-big-idea-improved-sound)), Chomsky
critiques the journalist by saying that Marr would not be placed where he is
placed unless he believed in certain ideas that the institutions that he was
way, like we have discussed another post, on how groups exert power. All
institutions exert power on individuals by setting a context within which the
members operate, just like Nature sets context via the forces of gravity,
electromagnetism, etc., and exerts the forces that leads to laws which we call
as the law of Nature that the individuals must abide by in order to survive.

Similarly institutions have rules said, or unsaid, which must be followed by
the members. If someone identifies these unspoken rules from the context and
starts questioning or defying them, they soon start being seen not as part of
the group. They are seen as a rogue agent are often mocked first, at least
lightly, to conform. If they don’t conform they are punished, if there are laws
of punishment within the group, if not, they are evicted from the group. We can
see the same phenomenon in Nature, assuming that someone does not confirm to
the law of nature they die or don’t procreate, similar to what happens in a
group that is either there is eviction or the rogue agents are not seen as a
part of the group.

What Chomsky is saying that in order to propagate their ideas without too much
trouble the institutions nap this problem in the bud and don’t let in the
members who don’t believe in the values that institutions would like to
propagate.  Furthermore, a Nature-based institution tries to imitate Nature as
closely as possible, would accept all living beings and the eviction from that
group would be coincident with the eviction from Nature, i.e. with death.
